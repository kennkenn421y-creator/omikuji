About Japanese Writing Systems

What is Katakana (カタカナ)?
Katakana is one of Japan's three writing systems, used primarily to write foreign words, names, and loanwords. It has angular, blocky shapes and is the standard way to write Western names in Japanese. When you see your name in Japan — on merchandise, in manga, or at hotels — it will almost always be in Katakana.
What is Hiragana (ひらがな)?
Hiragana is the softer, more cursive counterpart to Katakana. It is used for native Japanese words and grammatical elements. While names are usually written in Katakana, some people prefer the rounded look of Hiragana for artistic purposes. Both represent the same sounds.
How accurate is the conversion?
Japanese phonetics are syllable-based (mora system). English sounds that don't exist in Japanese are approximated — for example, "L" becomes "R", and consonant clusters get vowels inserted. The conversions shown here follow standard Japanese phonological conventions used in official transliteration guides.
